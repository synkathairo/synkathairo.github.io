Grünbaum's theorem

For any convex set 𝒮\mathcal{S} with center-of-gravity 𝐜\mathbf{c}, and any halfspace 𝒵={𝐱|⟨𝐚,𝐱−𝐜⟩≤0}\mathcal{Z}=\{\mathbf{x}|\langle\mathbf{a},\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{c}\rangle\leq 0\} then: vol(𝒮∩𝒵)vol(𝒮)≥1e≈.368\frac{\mathrm{vol}(\mathcal{S}\cap\mathcal{Z})}{\mathrm{vol}(\mathcal{S})} \geq \frac{1}{e} \approx .368

For any convex set K∈ℝnK \in \mathbb{R}^n with a center of gravity c∈ℝnc \in \mathbb{R}^n, and any halfspace H={x|a⊺(x−c)≥0}H = \{x | a^\intercal (x − c) ≥ 0\} passing through cc, 1e≤vol(K∩H)vol(K)≤(1−1e)\frac{1}{e} \leq \frac{\mathrm{vol}(K\cap H)}{\mathrm{vol}(K)} \leq \left(1-\frac{1}{e}\right)
