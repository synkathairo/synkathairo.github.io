Hahn-Banach theorem

Theorem, (ℝ\mathbb{R} version)

Let 𝒳\mathcal{X} be an ℝ\mathbb{R}-vector space. Suppose q:𝒳→ℝq : \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R} is a quasi-seminorm. Suppose also we are given a linear subspace 𝒴⊂𝒳\mathcal{Y} \subset \mathcal{X} and a linear map ϕ:𝒴→ℝ\phi : \mathcal{Y} \to \mathbb{R}, such that ϕ(y)≤q(y),∀y∈𝒴\phi(y) \leq q(y), \quad \forall y \in \mathcal{Y} Then there exists a linear map ψ:𝒳→ℝ\psi: \mathcal{X} \to \mathbb{R} such that ψ|𝒴=ϕ\psi |_\mathcal{Y} = \phi and ψ(x)≤q(x)\psi(x) \leq q(x) for all x∈𝒳x \in \mathcal{X}.
