A Convex function ff is α\alpha-strongly convex if, for all 𝐱,𝐲\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}: [f(𝐲)−f(𝐱)]−∇f(𝐱)𝖳(𝐲−𝐱)≥α2||𝐱−𝐲||22[f(\mathbf{y})-f(\mathbf{x})]-\nabla f(\mathbf{x})^\mathsf{T}(\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{x}) \geq \frac{\alpha}{2}||\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}||_2^2

Compare to smoothness condition [f(𝐲)−f(𝐱)]−∇f(𝐱)𝖳(𝐲−𝐱)≤β2||𝐱−𝐲||22[f(\mathbf{y})-f(\mathbf{x})]-\nabla f(\mathbf{x})^\mathsf{T}(\mathbf{y}-\mathbf{x}) \leq \frac{\beta}{2}||\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{y}||_2^2

For twice-differentiable scalar function ff, equivalent to f″(x)≥αf''(x) \geq \alpha .

When ff is convex, we always have that f″(x)≥0f''(x) ≥ 0, so larger values of αα correspond to a “stronger” condition.
