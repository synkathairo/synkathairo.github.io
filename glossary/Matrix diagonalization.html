Diagonalizable matrix

Consider a matrix AA that may be diagonalized. Then, there exists a nonsingular matrix TT such that T−1AT=ΛT^{-1} A T = \Lambda where Λ=diag(λ1...λn)\Lambda = \operatorname{diag}(\lambda_1...\lambda_n).
