Singular value decomposition

Without loss of generality, suppose that n≥dn \geq d. Any matrix 𝐗∈ℝn×d\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d} can be written in the form: $$\mathbf{X} = \mathbf{U}\mathbf{\Sigma}\mathbf{V}^{\sf T}$$

where 𝐔∈ℝn×d\mathbf{U} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times d}, 𝚺∈ℝd×d\mathbf{\Sigma} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}, 𝐕∈ℝd×d\mathbf{V} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}.

Denote the left singular vectors as columns in matrix 𝐔\mathbf{U} (n×dn \times d), singular values σ1,σ2,…,σd−1,σd\sigma_1, \sigma_2, …, \sigma_{d-1}, \sigma_d on the diagonal of matrix 𝚺\mathbf{\Sigma} (d×dd \times d), and right singular vectors as rows in matrix $\mathbf{V}^{\sf T}$ (d×dd \times d).

The following are satisfied: $\mathbf{U}^{\sf T} \mathbf{U} = \mathbf{I}$, $\mathbf{V}^{\sf T} \mathbf{V} = \mathbf{I}$, and σ1≥σ2≥…σd≥0\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq … \sigma_d \geq 0.

This is called the Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) of 𝐗\mathbf{X}.

  • The diagonals of 𝚺\mathbf{\Sigma} are called singular values of 𝐗\mathbf{X} (often sorted in decreasing order).
  • The columns of 𝐔\mathbf{U} are called the left singular vectors of 𝐗\mathbf{X}.
  • The columns of 𝐕\mathbf{V} are called the right singular vectors of 𝐗\mathbf{X}.
