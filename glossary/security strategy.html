security strategy

Definition

Consider a Matrix game (a Two player zero-sum game) where P1 is a minimizer, P2 is a maximizer.

P1 secures his strategy against any behavior of P2, picking row (i*i^*) whose largest entry is no bigger than the largest entry of any other row. The strategy "row ii" that secures losses no greater than V¯\overline{V} is the loss ceiling of P1, which satisfies V¯(A)=maxjai*j≤maxjaiji=1,...,m\overline{V}(A) = \max_j a_{i^* j} \leq \max_j a_{ij} \quad i=1,...,m or equivalently, the security level for his losses.

P2 will similarly secure gains against P1, and will choose the column (j*j^*) whose smallest entry is no smaller than the smallest entry from any other column, the gain-floor of P2 V_(A)=miniaij*≥miniaij\underline{V}(A) = \min_i a_{ij^*} \geq \min_i a_{ij} or equivalently, the security level for his gains.
