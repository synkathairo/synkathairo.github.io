chosen ciphertext attack security

Definition

An encryption scheme (E,D)(E,D) is chosen ciphertext attack (CCA) secure if every efficient adversary Mallory wins in the following game with probability at most 1/2+negl(n)1/2+negl(n):

  • Mallory chooses a pair of messages {m0,m1}{m0​,m1​}, a secret bb is chosen at random in {0,1}{0,1}, and Mallory gets c∗=Ek(mb)c∗=Ek​(mb​).
