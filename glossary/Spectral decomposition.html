Spectral decomposition

Symmetric matrices are always (orthogonally) diagonalizable.

That is, for any symmetric matrix 𝐀∈ℝn×n\mathbf{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}, there exists an orthogonal matrix 𝐐=[𝐪1...𝐪n]\mathbf{Q} = [\mathbf{q}_1...\mathbf{q}_n] and a diagonal matrix 𝚲=diag(λ1,…,λn)\mathbf{\Lambda} = \mathrm{diag} (\lambda_1,…,\lambda_n), both real and square, such that $$\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{Q\Lambda Q}^{\sf T}$$ where λi\lambda_i’s are the eigenvalues of 𝐀\mathbf{A} and 𝐪i\mathbf{q}_i’s the corresponding eigenvectors (orthogonal to each other and with unit norm).

Such a factorization is called the eigendecomposition of 𝐀\mathbf{A}, also called the spectral decomposition of 𝐀\mathbf{A}.

For general rectangular matrices, there is Singular value decomposition.
